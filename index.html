Section 1

The rise of the three-pointer

From a novelty line in 1979 to the engine of modern offenses, threes have skyrocketed in volume.

And, there is some truth in these critics. Since the introduction of the three pointer to the NBA in 1979, the average three point attempts per game during nba seasons has gradually increased. However, since 2011 and Steph Curry’s unanimous 2016 MVP season, the amount of threes taken per game has skyrocketed.

Section 2

Not all shots are created equal

Once you look at efficiency and expected points, the logic behind the three-point craze becomes clearer.

And this makes sense. Above are the shooting percentages by major shot type in the NBA in the 2024 season. On average, NBA players shot 78% at the free throw line, 66.3% on layups, 41.9% on midrange shots, and 36% on threes.

Once you convert those accuracies to the expected points per possession on these different shots, you can begin to understand why teams favor the three so much. Simply by stepping back a few steps, teams can increase their expected points per passion by .24! While free throws and layups are still the most valuable, threes are much more valuable than midranges for the average NBA player.

Section 3

Where did all the midrange shots go?

League-wide midrange volume has dropped to historic lows, but that doesn’t mean the shot has vanished.

Furthermore, when looking at the total midrange attempts taken year by year, the number of total midrange attempts has drastically fallen, reaching a lowest point in 2025. So are these NBA pundits and commentators correct? Has the midrange shot disappeared from the NBA? Is it a forgotten art and now the game is simply boring beyond belief?

Section 4

The midrange is now a star shot

Teams have shifted midrange usage away from role players and toward elite creators who can punish any coverage.

Not at all! Rather, the midrange is still prominently used among various NBA stars. For example, DeMar DeRozan in the 2024 season shot a whopping 640 midrange shots. Additionally, a recent NBA champion, Shai shot 348 attempts at 50% accuracy rate. Many of the stars in the NBA still utilize the midrange shot a lot! So then what is going on? Where did all the midrange shots disappear to?

Rather, what really happened is the midrange shot has disappeared for role players. Team have effectively shifted role players from taking catch and shoot long 2s to corner and above the break threes to capitalize on the larger expected points per possession. However, many stars still utilize and shoot the midranger exceptionally well. Rather, with the analytics revolution, coaches have realized that midrange shots have immense values, but only for incredible shooters and stars who can exert immense pressure on NBA defenses.

Section 5

Past vs present midrange specialists

The biggest change isn’t just volume — it’s shot selection. Modern stars are more selective and efficient than their predecessors.

This difference in approach towards who and when should midrange shots be taken can be shown through comparing the current NBA stars with the midrange stars of the past. As shown in the above graph, the top midrange stars in the 2004 season shot slightly more midrange shots. However, their accuracy is much lower. This illustrates how current stars are much more judicious about choosing when to shoot midrange shots. In our current NBA, the coaches may not even allow Iverson to shoot that many midrange shots!
